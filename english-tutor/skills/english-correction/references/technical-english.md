# Technical English Patterns for Developers

This reference covers English patterns specific to software development, programming, and technical communication.

## Code-Related Terminology

### Talking About Code

**Functions and Methods:**
- ✅ "The function **takes** two parameters"
- ✅ "The method **returns** a promise"
- ✅ "This function **accepts** an object"
- ✅ "The callback **receives** the result"

**Common mistakes:**
- ❌ "The function **gets** two parameters" (use "takes" or "accepts")
- ❌ "The function **gives** a value" (use "returns")

**Variables and Values:**
- ✅ "Declare a variable"
- ✅ "Initialize the variable **to** zero"
- ✅ "Set the value **to** null"
- ✅ "Assign a value **to** the variable"

**Data Structures:**
- ✅ "Add an element **to** the array"
- ✅ "Remove an item **from** the list"
- ✅ "The object **contains** a property"
- ✅ "Store data **in** the cache"

### Async/Promises

**Correct patterns:**
- ✅ "The function **is async**" OR "The function **is asynchronous**"
- ✅ "Wait **for** the promise **to resolve**"
- ✅ "The promise **resolves to** a value"
- ✅ "The promise **rejects with** an error"
- ✅ "Await the result"

**Common mistakes:**
- ❌ "The function is asynchronously"
- ❌ "Wait the promise"
- ❌ "The promise resolves a value"

### Error Handling

**Correct patterns:**
- ✅ "Throw an exception/error"
- ✅ "Catch the error"
- ✅ "Handle the exception"
- ✅ "The error **occurs** when..."
- ✅ "Raise an exception" (Python style)

**Common mistakes:**
- ❌ "Make an error"
- ❌ "Take an exception"
- ❌ "Get an error" (use "encounter" or "receive")

## API and Network

### HTTP Requests

**Verbs and prepositions:**
- ✅ "Make/Send a request **to** the endpoint"
- ✅ "Call the API"
- ✅ "Query the API"
- ✅ "POST data **to** the server"
- ✅ "GET data **from** the endpoint"
- ✅ "The endpoint **returns** JSON"
- ✅ "The API **responds with** data"

**Common mistakes:**
- ❌ "Call to the API" (omit "to")
- ❌ "Do a request" (use "make" or "send")
- ❌ "The endpoint **gives** JSON"

### REST API Terminology

**Resources:**
- ✅ "Create a resource"
- ✅ "Update the resource"
- ✅ "Delete the resource"
- ✅ "Fetch/Retrieve resources"

**HTTP Methods:**
- ✅ "Use GET **to** fetch data"
- ✅ "Send a POST request **to** create a resource"
- ✅ "Use PUT **to** update"
- ✅ "Use DELETE **to** remove"

**Status Codes:**
- ✅ "Return a 200 status code"
- ✅ "The server **returns** 404"
- ✅ "Get a 500 error"

## Database and Persistence

### Database Operations

**CRUD operations:**
- ✅ "Insert a record **into** the table"
- ✅ "Update a/the row"
- ✅ "Delete **from** the table"
- ✅ "Query the database"
- ✅ "Select records **from** the table"

**Common mistakes:**
- ❌ "Make a query" (use "run" or "execute")
- ❌ "Put a record" (use "insert")
- ❌ "Take from database" (use "retrieve" or "fetch")

### Transactions

**Correct patterns:**
- ✅ "Start/Begin a transaction"
- ✅ "Commit the transaction"
- ✅ "Roll back the transaction"
- ✅ "The transaction **fails**"

### Schema and Models

**Correct patterns:**
- ✅ "Define a schema"
- ✅ "The table **has** three columns"
- ✅ "The column **stores** timestamps"
- ✅ "Add a foreign key **to** the table"

## Testing

### Test Terminology

**Writing tests:**
- ✅ "Write a test **for** the function"
- ✅ "Add test coverage **for** edge cases"
- ✅ "Mock the dependency"
- ✅ "Stub the method"
- ✅ "Spy on the function"

**Running tests:**
- ✅ "Run the tests"
- ✅ "Execute the test suite"
- ✅ "The test **passes**" (not "succeeds")
- ✅ "The test **fails**" (not "loses")
- ✅ "All tests are **passing**"

**Common mistakes:**
- ❌ "Make a test" (use "write" or "create")
- ❌ "The test success"
- ❌ "Do tests"

### Assertions

**Correct patterns:**
- ✅ "Assert that the value **is** correct"
- ✅ "Expect the function **to return** null"
- ✅ "Verify that the method **was called**"
- ✅ "Check **if** the result **is** truthy"

## Git and Version Control

### Commits

**Imperative mood (standard):**
- ✅ "Add feature X"
- ✅ "Fix bug in Y"
- ✅ "Update documentation"
- ✅ "Remove deprecated code"

**Common mistakes:**
- ❌ "Added feature X" (past tense)
- ❌ "Adds feature X" (present tense)
- ❌ "Adding feature X" (-ing form)

**Articles in commits (often omitted):**
- ✅ "Fix bug in authentication" (no "the" or "a")
- ✅ "Add user validation" (no article)
- Also acceptable: "Fix the authentication bug" (with article)

### Repository Operations

**Correct patterns:**
- ✅ "Clone the repository"
- ✅ "Fork the repo"
- ✅ "Push **to** the repository"
- ✅ "Pull **from** upstream"
- ✅ "Merge the branch **into** main"
- ✅ "Create a branch"
- ✅ "Check out a branch"

**Common mistakes:**
- ❌ "Push the repository"
- ❌ "Merge the branch **to** main" (use "into")

### Pull Requests

**Correct patterns:**
- ✅ "Open/Create a pull request"
- ✅ "Review the PR"
- ✅ "Approve the changes"
- ✅ "Request changes"
- ✅ "Merge the PR **into** main"

## Build and Deployment

### Building

**Correct patterns:**
- ✅ "Build the project"
- ✅ "Compile the code"
- ✅ "Bundle the application"
- ✅ "The build **succeeds**" OR "The build **is successful**"
- ✅ "The build **fails**"

### Deployment

**Correct patterns:**
- ✅ "Deploy **to** production"
- ✅ "Deploy **to** the cloud"
- ✅ "Roll out the update"
- ✅ "Ship the feature"
- ✅ "Release version 2.0"

**Common mistakes:**
- ❌ "Deploy **on** production" (use "to")
- ❌ "Deploy the production"

### CI/CD

**Correct patterns:**
- ✅ "Run the pipeline"
- ✅ "The pipeline **passes**"
- ✅ "Trigger a build"
- ✅ "The workflow **runs** on push"

## Code Review

### Giving Feedback

**Constructive patterns:**
- ✅ "Consider using..."
- ✅ "This could be improved **by**..."
- ✅ "Have you thought about..."
- ✅ "Suggest adding..."

**Pointing out issues:**
- ✅ "This might cause..."
- ✅ "There's a potential issue with..."
- ✅ "Watch out for..."

**Approving:**
- ✅ "Looks good **to me**" (LGTM)
- ✅ "This looks great"
- ✅ "Nice work"

## Common Technical Abbreviations

**Proper usage:**
- ✅ "The API" (say "the" before API)
- ✅ "An HTML file" (use "an" because H is pronounced "aitch")
- ✅ "A URL" (use "a" because U is pronounced "you")
- ✅ "An SQL query" (use "an" because S is pronounced "ess")

**Pronunciation affects article choice:**
- If abbreviation starts with vowel sound → "an"
- If abbreviation starts with consonant sound → "a"

## Tech Stack Terminology

### Frameworks and Libraries

**Correct patterns:**
- ✅ "Use React **for** the frontend"
- ✅ "Built **with** Node.js"
- ✅ "Based **on** Express"
- ✅ "Integrate **with** a third-party library"
- ✅ "Add a dependency **to** the project"

**No articles with names:**
- ✅ "React is a framework" (not "The React")
- ✅ "Node.js handles backend" (not "The Node.js")

### Cloud and Infrastructure

**Correct patterns:**
- ✅ "Deploy **to** AWS"
- ✅ "Host **on** Heroku"
- ✅ "Run **in** a container"
- ✅ "Use Docker **for** containerization"
- ✅ "Set up **on** Kubernetes"

## Meetings and Standups

### Status Updates

**Correct patterns:**
- ✅ "I'm **working on** X"
- ✅ "I **finished** X" OR "I've **finished** X"
- ✅ "I **plan to** work on Y"
- ✅ "I **need help with** Z"

**Blockers:**
- ✅ "I'm **blocked by** X"
- ✅ "I'm **waiting for** Y"
- ✅ "I **ran into** an issue"

### Scheduling

**Correct patterns:**
- ✅ "Schedule a meeting **for** tomorrow"
- ✅ "Let's meet **at** 2pm"
- ✅ "Available **on** Monday"
- ✅ "Can you join **in** 10 minutes?"

## Project Management

### Tickets and Issues

**Correct patterns:**
- ✅ "Create a ticket **for** this bug"
- ✅ "Assign the issue **to** someone"
- ✅ "Close the ticket"
- ✅ "Reopen the issue"
- ✅ "Link the PR **to** the ticket"

### Sprints and Agile

**Correct patterns:**
- ✅ "Plan the sprint"
- ✅ "Add **to** the backlog"
- ✅ "Move the ticket **to** 'In Progress'"
- ✅ "The sprint **ends** on Friday"

## Quick Reference

**Most common technical verb + preposition pairs:**
- Connect **to**
- Integrate **with**
- Deploy **to**
- Based **on**
- Built **with**
- Work **on** (a project)
- Work **with** (a team)
- Push **to**
- Pull **from**
- Merge **into**
- Add **to**
- Remove **from**

**Most common technical collocations:**
- Run tests
- Write code
- Fix bugs
- Deploy to production
- Build the project
- Review the PR
- Merge the branch
- Query the database
- Call the API

Focus on these patterns and most technical English will flow naturally.
